## Root Cause

KZ Član 208 (prevara/fraud) does **not** appear in the top-10 semantic search results for "Kazna za prevaru iznad milion dinara?" because of a penalty-threshold phrasing mismatch. The user's query says "milion dinara" (1M RSD), but KZ 208's qualifying clause reads "milion **i petsto hiljada** dinara" (1.5M RSD). Meanwhile, dozens of other KZ articles use the exact phrase "milion dinara" in their penalty clauses (Čl. 379, 301, 414, 363, 224, 208b, 217, 214, 227, 201…), all scoring higher on the embedding. KZ 208's primary definition text (st. 1: "lažnim prikazivanjem ili prikrivanjem činjenica u zabludu") is semantically distant from "milion dinara". The article never appears in the retrieval pool, so no reranker or hint can fix it.

Secondary issue: there is also a KZ Član 208b (insurance fraud) with similar structure. Without a word-boundary check, any "208 in article" test matches both 208 and 208b.

---

## Approach Chosen

**Semantic expansion injection** (`_jeden_retrieval_krug` + `_izracunaj_skor` + post-Cohere override) — Approach B variant using a semantically crafted expansion term rather than a zero-vector direct fetch.

Direct fetch (`_direktan_fetch_clana` with zero vector) was ruled out because Pinecone returns `score=0.0` for zero-vector queries, giving LOW confidence in the pipeline (score < 0.52 threshold). A LOW-confidence response gives a disclaimer answer, not the specific penalty the user needs.

Instead, a targeted semantic expansion term `"krivično delo prevare lažnim prikazivanjem imovinska korist KZ 208"` is used. This term is semantically close to KZ 208's st. 1 definition text (shares "lažnim prikazivanjem" and "imovinska korist"), so it retrieves KZ 208 with a real cosine score (~0.65+), yielding HIGH confidence and a specific legal answer.

The `_izracunaj_skor` boost (+80) ensures KZ 208 ranks above KZ 379 (base score ~67) in the internal candidate ordering → enters Cohere's top-k input. The post-Cohere override is the final safety net in case Cohere still ranks another article first.

All three triggers use `\bprevar` (6-char stem) to match "prevara", "prevaru", "prevare", "prevari" — the accusative form "prevaru" in Q5 requires the stem rather than the full word.
